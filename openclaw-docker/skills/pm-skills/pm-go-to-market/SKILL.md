# Go-to-Market for FinTech

This skill collection helps FinTech PMs plan and execute go-to-market strategies with attention to trust-building, regulatory positioning, and financial customer acquisition nuances.

## Included Skills

### 1. ideal-customer-profile — Define ICP
Identify your Ideal Customer Profile for FinTech products.

**FinTech ICP Dimensions:**

**Demographics (Firmographic):**
- Company size (revenue, employees)
- Industry vertical (e-commerce, SaaS, retail, etc.)
- Transaction volume / GMV
- Geographic markets (regulatory considerations)
- Current payment/financial stack

**Behaviors (Financial):**
- Current payment methods used
- Frustration with existing solutions
- Risk tolerance for new financial providers
- Decision-making process for financial tools
- Integration requirements

**Jobs to Be Done (JTBD):**
- Primary: "When I'm [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [outcome]"
- Financial JTBD examples:
  - "When checkout is abandoned, I want to offer flexible payment, so I can recover lost sales"
  - "When managing cash flow, I want to defer payments, so I can invest in inventory"

**Trust Signals:**
- What makes them trust a new financial provider?
- What security certifications do they look for?
- What social proof influences their decision?

**Example Usage:**
```
Use skill: ideal-customer-profile
Args: BNPL solution for mid-market e-commerce merchants
```

### 2. beachhead-segment — Select Beachhead Market
Identify the first market segment to dominate.

**FinTech Beachhead Criteria:**

**Burning Pain:**
- High cart abandonment due to lack of payment options
- Cash flow constraints preventing inventory purchases
- High chargeback rates with current processor
- Complex international payment acceptance

**Willingness to Pay:**
- Clear ROI calculation (increased conversion, reduced fraud)
- Budget allocated to payment/financial solutions
- Price sensitivity lower for revenue-generating tools

**Winnable Market Share:**
- Underserved niche (specific vertical, geography)
- Limited competition from incumbents
- Your product has clear differentiation

**Referral Potential:**
- Industry communities (e-commerce forums, fintech groups)
- Agency/consultant relationships
- Platform partnerships (Shopify, WooCommerce, etc.)

**FinTech Beachhead Example:**
```markdown
## Recommended Beachhead: Mid-market Fashion E-commerce

**Why This Segment:**
- High cart abandonment (average 70%+) = burning pain
- Strong margins allow BNPL fees (willingness to pay)
- Fragmented market, no dominant BNPL player (winnable)
- Active community on Shopify/Instagram (referral potential)

**Entry Strategy:**
1. Build Shopify app for easy integration
2. Target 50 merchants through direct outreach
3. Offer revenue-share model to reduce friction
4. Generate case studies showing conversion lift
5. Expand to adjacent verticals (beauty, accessories)
```

### 3. gtm-strategy — Create GTM Strategy
Develop comprehensive go-to-market plans.

**FinTech GTM Components:**

**Trust-First Messaging:**
- Security certifications (PCI DSS, SOC 2)
- Regulatory compliance (licenses, registrations)
- Customer protection policies
- Transparent pricing (no hidden fees)

**Channel Strategy:**
- **Partners:** Platform integrations (Shopify, Salesforce)
- **Inbound:** SEO for payment/BNPL keywords
- **Outbound:** Targeted ABM for enterprise
- **Community:** Developer docs, API references

**Success Metrics:**
- Merchant signups / activations
- Transaction volume (GMV)
- Take rate (revenue / GMV)
- Net revenue retention
- Payback period (CAC vs LTV)

**Launch Phases:**
1. **Beta:** Limited merchants, manual onboarding
2. **Soft Launch:** Self-serve, select geographies
3. **General Availability:** Full marketing push
4. **Scale:** Enterprise sales, international expansion

**Regulatory Considerations:**
- License requirements by market
- Marketing compliance (no misleading claims)
- Disclosure requirements (terms, fees, APR)

### 4. gtm-motions — Select GTM Motions
Choose the right mix of acquisition channels.

**FinTech GTM Motion Fit:**

| Motion | Fit for FinTech | Best For |
|--------|-----------------|----------|
| **Inbound** | High | Building trust through education |
| **Outbound** | Medium-High | Enterprise/B2B sales |
| **Paid Digital** | Medium | Targeted acquisition, retargeting |
| **Community** | High | Developer/API products |
| **Partners** | Very High | Platform integrations, distribution |
| **ABM** | High | Enterprise financial institutions |
| **PLG** | Medium-High | Self-serve SMB products |

**FinTech-Specific Tactics:**

**Partner Motion (Highest Priority):**
- Platform app stores (Shopify, Salesforce)
- Banking-as-a-Service partnerships
- Referral partnerships with complementary tools
- System integrator relationships

**Inbound Motion:**
- Content: "BNPL compliance guide", "Fraud prevention playbook"
- SEO: High-intent keywords ("best BNPL for Shopify")
- Webinars: Industry trends, compliance updates

**Community Motion:**
- Developer documentation and APIs
- GitHub repositories with sample code
- Discord/Slack communities for fintech builders

### 5. competitive-battlecard — Competitive Positioning
Create competitive positioning for sales and marketing.

**FinTech Battlecard Elements:**
- Regulatory advantages (licenses, compliance)
- Security differentiators
- Pricing transparency
- Integration ease
- Customer support quality

## FinTech GTM Checklist

### Pre-Launch
- [ ] Regulatory licenses obtained
- [ ] Security certifications (PCI DSS, SOC 2)
- [ ] Legal review of all marketing materials
- [ ] Customer support team trained
- [ ] Risk/fraud systems operational
- [ ] Banking partnerships established

### Launch
- [ ] Trust signals prominent (security badges, certifications)
- [ ] Clear pricing and terms
- [ ] Easy integration path
- [ ] Case studies/social proof ready
- [ ] Compliance documentation accessible

### Post-Launch
- [ ] Monitor fraud rates closely
- [ ] Track customer satisfaction (NPS)
- [ ] Iterate on onboarding friction
- [ ] Expand to adjacent segments
